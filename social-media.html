Social Media Marketing Basics

Pick the right platforms, plan a content calendar, and grow an audience organically.

Published: July 18, 2026

Choosing the Right Platforms

You don't need to be everywhere. Pick 1–2 platforms where your audience already spends time:

  • Instagram — visual products, lifestyle, younger audiences.
  • LinkedIn — B2B, professional services, career-related content.
  • YouTube — tutorials, in-depth explanations, long-term discoverability.
  • TikTok — short-form, entertainment-driven, fast growth potential.

Building a Content Calendar

Plan content a week or two in advance instead of posting randomly. A simple structure that works well for beginners:

  • Educational — tips, how-tos, common mistakes.
  • Behind-the-scenes — your process, your story.
  • Engagement — questions, polls, opinions.
  • Promotional — your product or service (keep this to a small share).

Growing an Audience Organically

Consistency matters more than perfection. Posting regularly — even 2-3 times a week — builds momentum faster than sporadic high-effort posts. Engage with comments and other accounts in your niche; algorithms tend to reward accounts that generate real conversation.

What to Track

Ignore vanity metrics like follower count early on. Instead, watch engagement rate (likes, comments, shares relative to reach) and saves/shares, which usually signal genuinely useful content.

Getting Started — Quick Steps

  1. Pick one platform to focus on first.
  2. Define 3–4 content categories to rotate through.
  3. Plan a 2-week content calendar.
  4. Post consistently and engage with comments daily.
  5. Review what performs best after a month, then double down on it.
