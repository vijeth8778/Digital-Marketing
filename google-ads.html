Google Ads for Beginners

A simple guide to understanding campaign types, keyword bidding, and Quality Score.

Published: July 12, 2026

What is Google Ads?

Google Ads is an advertising platform that lets you show ads on Google Search, YouTube, and millions of partner websites. You pay only when someone interacts with your ad — usually by clicking it.

Types of Google Ads Campaigns

There are several campaign types to choose from depending on your goal:

  • Search campaigns — text ads shown in Google search results.
  • Display campaigns — image ads shown on websites and apps.
  • Video campaigns — ads shown on YouTube.
  • Shopping campaigns — product listings shown in search results.

How Keyword Bidding Works

You choose keywords related to your business, and set a bid — the maximum amount you're willing to pay per click. Google then runs an auction each time someone searches that keyword, factoring in your bid and your ad quality.

What is Quality Score?

Quality Score is Google's rating (1–10) of how relevant and useful your ad is to someone searching. It's based on expected click-through rate, ad relevance, and landing page experience. A higher Quality Score can lower your cost per click.

Launching Your First Ad — Quick Steps

  1. Create a Google Ads account.
  2. Choose your campaign goal (e.g. website traffic, leads, sales).
  3. Select a campaign type (Search is best for beginners).
  4. Pick relevant keywords for your business.
  5. Write your ad headline and description.
  6. Set your daily budget and launch.

Start small — even a $5–10 daily budget is enough to learn how the platform responds to your keywords and ad copy before scaling up.
