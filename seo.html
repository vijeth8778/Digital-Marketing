SEO for Beginners

Learn the basics of SEO, keyword research, on-page SEO and technical SEO.

Published: July 21, 2026 | Updated: July 22, 2026

What is SEO?

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of improving your website so it ranks higher in search results — like Google — without paying for ads. Higher rankings mean more free, ongoing traffic to your site.

Keyword Research

Keyword research means finding the exact words and phrases people type into search engines when looking for something related to your business. Free tools like Google Keyword Planner or Ubersuggest can help you find keywords with good search volume and manageable competition.

On-Page SEO

On-page SEO covers everything you control directly on your website:

  • Title tags — include your main keyword near the front.
  • Meta descriptions — a short summary that encourages clicks from search results.
  • Headings (H1, H2) — structure your content clearly for readers and search engines.
  • Internal links — link to other relevant pages on your own site.

Technical SEO Basics

Technical SEO makes sure search engines can actually find and understand your site. Key basics include:

  • Fast page load speed.
  • Mobile-friendly design.
  • A clean site structure with working links (no broken pages).
  • A sitemap.xml submitted to Google Search Console.

Getting Started — Quick Steps

  1. Pick 3–5 target keywords relevant to your business.
  2. Write a page or article optimized around one keyword each.
  3. Set up Google Search Console to monitor how you're ranking.
  4. Fix any broken links or slow-loading pages.
  5. Keep publishing consistent, useful content over time.

SEO is a long game — most results build up over months, not days. Consistency matters more than any single "trick."
