Email Marketing 101

Build a list, write emails people open, and set up your first automated welcome sequence.

Published: July 16, 2026

Why Email Marketing Still Works

Unlike social media, your email list is something you own — no algorithm decides who sees your message. It's often one of the highest-return marketing channels because you're reaching people who already chose to hear from you.

Building Your List

Start by offering something valuable in exchange for an email address — a checklist, a discount, a free guide, or early access to something. This is usually called a "lead magnet." Add a signup form to your website, blog posts, and social profiles.

Choosing an Email Platform

Beginner-friendly platforms include Mailchimp, MailerLite, and ConvertKit. Most offer free plans up to a certain number of subscribers, so you can start without any cost.

Writing Emails People Actually Open

  • Subject line — keep it short, specific, and curiosity-driven.
  • Preview text — the snippet shown next to the subject line; don't waste it.
  • One clear goal per email — don't try to sell five things at once.
  • Write like a person — conversational tone outperforms overly formal copy.

Setting Up a Welcome Sequence

A welcome sequence is a short series of automated emails sent to new subscribers. A simple 3-email version works well for beginners:

  1. Email 1 (immediately) — deliver what you promised, introduce yourself.
  2. Email 2 (2 days later) — share your best free content or a helpful resource.
  3. Email 3 (4–5 days later) — introduce your product, service, or offer.

Key Metrics to Track

Focus on open rate (are your subject lines working?) and click rate (is your content driving action?). Don't worry too much about list size early on — a smaller, engaged list outperforms a large, inactive one.
